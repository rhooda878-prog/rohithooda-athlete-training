6-Week Sample — Physical Fitness & Conditioning

Designed to build aerobic fitness, functional strength and mobility. Train 4–5 days/week. Progress load or reps each week.

Week 1 — Baseline & Technique

  • Day 1: Fitness test (1.6km run or 2km for selection standards), push-up & squat max, plank hold; light aerobic 20–30 min
  • Day 2: Strength (bodyweight focus) — squats 3x12, push-ups 3x10, lunges 3x10 each, core circuit 3 rounds
  • Day 3: Active recovery — mobility & stretching, brisk walk 30 min
  • Day 4: Interval running — 6x400m with 90s rest (easy pace)
  • Day 5: Full-body conditioning — circuit 5 rounds (burpees, mountain climbers, jump squats, rows)

Week 2 — Build Volume

  • Increase interval sets or reduce rest; add 1–2 reps per strength exercise; include hill sprints (6–8 x 50–80m)

Week 3 — Strength Emphasis

  • Introduce loaded strength (if gym available): deadlift/romanian deadlift 4x6, goblet squat 4x8, bench/press variations 4x6; maintain aerobic intervals

Week 4 — Speed & Power

  • Plyometrics, sprint mechanics, short max-effort sprints (8–10 x 40m), explosive strength (box jumps, medicine ball throws)

Week 5 — Specificity

  • Simulate selection tests: timed runs, shuttle runs, long jump practice; strength maintenance sets and mobility sessions

Week 6 — Test & Taper

  • Reduce volume, maintain intensity. Retest fitness markers (run time, push-ups, plank). Review results and set next-phase targets.

Example Daily Session (Intermediate)

  1. Warm-up: 10 min mobility + dynamic drills
  2. Strength: 30–40 min (compound lifts or bodyweight progressions)
  3. Conditioning: Intervals or circuits 15–20 min
  4. Cool-down: Stretching and breathing work 8–10 min

Notes: plans are individualized based on age, baseline fitness and medical clearance. Nutrition and recovery guidance included with all packages.
